An engineering education loan is a sum of money borrowed to finance higher education-related expenses while pursuing a BTech or BE degree. These loans cover the cost of tuition, books & supplies, and living expenses while the student is in the process of pursuing a degree. Education loans are of different types.
The applicant must apply for an education loan with a co-applicant. The co-applicant can be a parent, guardian or spouse in case the applicant is married. Interest rate is low if the amount is less than INR 4 lakh. Interest rates increase with the amount being borrowed.

Engineering loan imortants points

Indian students between the ages of 16 years and 35 years can avail an education loan from a registered lender. These can be for pursuing graduate, post graduate, or diploma courses in several engineering branches. To do so, the applicant must have a confirmed admission (with document proof) in a university or a college recognized by the UGC, AICTE, and other such boards. The applicant must apply for an education loan with a co-applicant. The co-applicant can be a parent, guardian or spouse in case the applicant is married. Interest rate is low if the amount is less than INR 4 lakh. Interest rates increase with the amount being borrowed. Also, the education loan interest rate will vary as per the bank and is subject to frequent changes. Many banks offer concessions in interest rates for female students who have secured admission to recognized engineering colleges. Most banks also do not demand security for education loan amounts up to INR 4 lakh. However, for amounts in the range of INR 10 lakh to INR 30 lakh and above, documents of assets like gold and houses can be pledged as security.
